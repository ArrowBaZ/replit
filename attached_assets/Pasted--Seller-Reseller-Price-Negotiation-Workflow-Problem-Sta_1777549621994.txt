
# Seller-Reseller Price Negotiation Workflow

## Problem Statement / Motivation

Currently, when a reseller submits articles to a seller at proposed prices, the seller has no way to:
- Accept articles at the proposed prices
- Reject articles
- Counter-propose alternative prices
- Engage in multi-round price negotiation

This blocks the article validation workflow and forces sellers into manual communication channels (email/chat), creating friction and incomplete request tracking. Articles submitted by resellers remain in a limbo state with no formal acceptance or renegotiation path.

## Overview

This feature adds a price negotiation interface where sellers can:
- **View pending articles** submitted by resellers with proposed prices
- **Accept articles** in bulk or individually at offered prices
- **Counter-propose prices** on individual articles, initiating negotiation
- **Track negotiation history** showing all offers and counter-offers
- Resellers can then **accept or counter-propose** in response

Articles transition through states: `pending â†’ negotiating â†’ accepted/rejected/finalized`

## Proposed Solution

1. **Seller Article Review Page**: A dedicated view showing all pending articles from active requests, grouped by reseller with price summaries
2. **Item-Level Actions**: For each article, show current proposed price with action buttons:
   - "Accept" â€” Confirm at current price
   - "Counter-Offer" â€” Propose alternative price (opens modal with price input)
   - "Reject" â€” Decline the article
3. **Bulk Actions**: "Accept All" to confirm all articles in a request at once
4. **Negotiation Timeline**: Show price history when an article is being negotiated (who proposed what price and when)
5. **State Tracking**: Articles persist in the database with negotiation records for audit/history
6. **Notifications**: Notify resellers when seller counters a price; notify seller when reseller responds

## Technical Considerations

- **Database schema changes**: Add `article_negotiations` table to track price history; add `status` field to articles (`pending`, `accepted`, `rejected`, `negotiating`)
- **API endpoints**: 
  - `POST /articles/:id/accept` â€” Seller accepts article at current price
  - `POST /articles/:id/reject` â€” Seller rejects article
  - `POST /articles/:id/counter-offer` â€” Seller proposes new price, creates negotiation record
  - `GET /articles?status=pending` â€” Fetch pending articles for review
  - `GET /articles/:id/negotiations` â€” Fetch negotiation history
- **Real-time updates**: Consider WebSocket or polling for live negotiation state (both parties seeing offers in real-time)
- **Price validation**: Ensure counter-proposed prices fall within tier constraints (respect the existing fee tier logic)

## System-Wide Impact

### Interaction graph
- When seller accepts â†’ reseller receives notification â†’ they can upload final files
- When seller counters â†’ creates negotiation record â†’ reseller can view and respond
- Reseller response triggers another notification to seller

### Error propagation
- If price counter-offer violates fee tier constraints, return 400 with tier violation message; seller sees inline error

### State lifecycle risks
- **Risk**: Orphaned negotiations if an article is deleted while negotiating. **Mitigation**: Soft-delete articles, mark as `rejected`
- **Risk**: Multiple simultaneous counter-offers from both sides race condition. **Mitigation**: Use optimistic locking or version field on article

### API surface parity
- Other parts of the app viewing articles will need to handle new `status` field; consider adding status badges/labels throughout UI

## Success Metrics

- Negotiation flow supports 2+ rounds of counter-offers without errors
- Article state correctly persists and is visible to both parties
- Audit trail shows full negotiation history for compliance

## Dependencies & Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Race condition on simultaneous counter-offers | Data corruption, unclear state | Add version/timestamp field; last-write-wins or optimistic locking |
| Seller/reseller both propose new price at same time | Ambiguous negotiation state | Queue offers, apply sequentially; notify party of conflict |
| Fee tier constraint violations on counter-offer | Invalid final price accepted | Validate every counter-offer against fee tiers before persisting |
| No pricing logic exists to auto-reject if prices don't meet min/max | Endless negotiation | Implement tier-aware validation + optional auto-reject if outside range |
| Resellers don't see counter-offer notifications | Negotiation stalls silently | Ensure email/in-app notifications trigger on every counter-offer |
